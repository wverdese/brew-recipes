# 🍺 Alcoholic Ginger Beer w/ Ginger Bug (5L, 4–5% ABV, Sweet-Spicy Balance)

## 🐛 Ginger Bug

### Goal

* Start: 250 ml bug, ~45g ginger
* End: ~200 ml active bug
* Use: 150 ml liquid (after straining)

### Day 1 (Start)

* Water: 250 ml (20–25°C)
* Ginger: 15 g (finely grated, unpeeled)
* Rørsukker: 15 g

Mix in a clean jar.
Cover loosely (not airtight).
Keep at ~20–22°C.

### Day 2-6 (Steps)

* Ginger: 8 g
* Rørsukker: 8 g

Add and stir well.

### Readiness Check

Bug is ready when:

* Bubbles form within 2–6 hours after feeding
* Slight foam layer present
* Smells fresh, gingery, lightly yeasty
* No rotten or foul odors

### Pre-Brew Activation (Important)

12–24h before brew day:

* Ginger: 10 g
* Rørsukker: 10 g

Ensures strong fermentation activity.

### Straining (Brew Day)

1. Stir bug well
2. Wait 10–20 seconds
3. Strain through cheesecloth (do NOT squeeze hard)

Collect:

* 150 ml liquid for pitching
* Optional: keep leftovers as ongoing culture

## 🍺 Brew Day

### Target

* Batch Size: 5 L
* OG: ~1.070
* Bottle at: ~1.015–1.020
* ABV: ~4–5%
* Sweetness: Medium → adjusted with stevia
* Carbonation: High

---

### Ingredients

* Rørsukker: 400 g
* Brun farin: 600 g
* Fresh øko ginger: 300 g
* Lime: 1 (zest + juice)
* Salt: 0.5–1 g
* Water: ~6 L
* Ginger bug: 150 ml (active)
* Stevia extract: to taste

---

### Ginger Prep

* 200 g ginger → boil portion
* 100 g ginger → late steep

Grate or finely chop (no peeling needed)

### 1. Boil

Add to pot:

* 5.5–6 L water
* 200 g ginger
* 400 g rørsukker
* 600 g brun farin

Bring to boil, then simmer **30 minutes**.

---

### 2. Flameout / Steep

Turn off heat. Add:

* 100 g ginger
* Zest of 1 lime

Steep **20 minutes**.

---

### 3. Cooling

Cool to **20–22°C**.

Add:

* Juice of 1 lime
* 0.5–1 g salt

---

### 4. Fermentation

* Transfer to fermenter
* Add 150 ml ginger bug
* Ferment at **20–22°C**

---

## Fermentation Timeline

| Day | Stage               |
| --- | ------------------- |
| 0   | Pitch               |
| 1   | Lag phase           |
| 2–3 | Active fermentation |
| 4–6 | Slowing down        |

Start tasting from Day 4.

---

## Bottling (Critical)

Bottle when:

* Noticeably less sweet
* Still some sugar remaining
* Light fermentation ongoing

**Typically Day 4–6**
Target gravity: **1.015–1.020**

Add priming sugar:

* 3–4 g/L → **15–20 g total**

Bottle in strong bottles + 1 PET bottle.

---

## Carbonation

* Store at 20–22°C
* Monitor PET bottle

Ready when:
→ Firm like soda (1–3 days)

---

## Cold Storage

Move to fridge (≤4°C) immediately after carbonation.

---

## Stevia Adjustment

### Best (per glass)

* Add 1 drop
* Stir gently
* Taste and adjust

### Calibration

* Test in 100 ml sample first

---

## Conditioning & Safety

* Carbonation: 1–3 days
* Drink within: 1–3 weeks
* Always store cold

**Important:**

* Do not leave bottles warm long-term
* Use PET bottle to monitor pressure
* Open carefully (high carbonation)

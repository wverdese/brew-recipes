# 🍺 Aurora Oro – Nordic Doppio Malto with Kveik

*(21L Braumeister Version, Crystal Clear Golden Ale)*

A Nordic-Italian hybrid: continental malt character, restrained noble hops, and the practicality of Kveik fermentation. Designed to be affordable, easy to brew, and highly drinkable.

## Target Stats

* **OG:** ~1.058
* **FG:** ~1.010–1.012
* **ABV:** ~5.9–6.1%
* **IBU:** ~22–24
* **EBC:** ~11–12
* **Color:** `#D1A247` $$\color{#D1A247}{\blacksquare}$$


---

## Grain Bill (Braumeister-friendly)

| Grain            |  Amount |   % |
| ---------------- | ------: | --: |
| BEST Vienna Malt | 3.50 kg | 58% |
| BEST Pilsen Malt | 2.50 kg | 42% |

---

## Water

### Profile (recommended)

* **Ca:** 50–70 ppm
* **Mg:** 5–10 ppm
* **Cl:** 50–60 ppm
* **SO₄:** 50–70 ppm

### pH

* Target **5.3–5.4**

---

## Recipe

### Mash Schedule

| Minute | Step              | Target Temp (°C) | Action / Notes                  |
| ------ | ----------------- | ---------------- | ------------------------------- |
| 0      | Heat Strike Water | ~52              | Preheat before dough-in         |
| 1      | Dough-in          | 52               | Add grains and stir thoroughly  |
| 1–10   | Ramp              | 64               | Controlled rise                 |
| 10–60  | Saccharification  | 64               | Promote fermentability          |
| 60–75  | Malt Rest         | 68               | Develop body and malt character |
| 75–85  | Mash-out Ramp     | 75               | Raise temperature               |
| 85–95  | Mash-out          | 75               | Improve lautering               |
| 95     | End Mash          | —                | Remove malt pipe                |

#### Notes

* Vienna malt provides bread crust, biscuit and light honey notes.
* Pilsner malt keeps the beer bright, crisp and highly drinkable.

---

### Lauter / Sparge

* Sparge with **75°C** water.
* Collect approximately **26 L** pre-boil.
* Expected pre-boil gravity: **~1.051**

---

### Hop Schedule (~23 IBU)

| Minute | Stage | Temperature | Hop Addition              | Notes                        |
| ------ | ----- | ----------- | ------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| 60     | Boil  | 100°C       | 25 g Hallertau Mittelfrüh | Main bittering addition      |
| 15     | Boil  | 100°C       | 15 g Hallertau Mittelfrüh | Light noble aroma and flavor |
| 15     | Boil  | 100°C       | 1 Protafloc tablet        | Clarity aid                  |

---

### Cooling

* Chill to **20°C**
* Transfer to fermenter
* Aerate thoroughly

---

### Fermentation

* **Yeast:** Mangrove Jack's M12 Kveik
* **Pitch Temperature:** 20°C
* **Primary Fermentation:** 20–22°C
* **Cleanup:** Hold at 22°C until FG is stable
* **Cold Crash:** 2–4°C for 3–5 days

---

### Fermentation Timeline (Approx.)

| Day  | Stage               | Temp    | SG (approx.) | Notes                |
| ---- | ------------------- | ------- | ------------ | -------------------- |
| 0    | Pitch yeast         | 20°C    | 1.058        | Oxygenate well       |
| 1–2  | Active fermentation | 20–22°C | 1.035–1.045  | Strong activity      |
| 3–4  | Main attenuation    | 20–22°C | 1.018–1.022  | Most sugars consumed |
| 5–6  | Near FG             | 22°C    | 1.011–1.013  | Cleanup phase        |
| 7    | Stable FG           | 22°C    | 1.010–1.012  | Ready for cold crash |
| 8–12 | Cold conditioning   | 2–4°C   | Stable       | Beer clears rapidly  |

---

### Packaging

* Aim for **2.4–2.5 vol CO₂**
* Serve at **6–8°C**

---

### 🧊 Conditioning Time – Overview

| Method      | Conditioning Time  | Notes                             |
| ----------- | ------------------ | --------------------------------- |
| **Kegged**  | 1–2 weeks          | Excellent after cold conditioning |
| **Bottled** | 2–3 weeks at ~20°C | Chill 48h before serving          |

---

## Final Notes

✅ Only two malts, one hop, one yeast.

✅ Crystal-clear appearance is very achievable thanks to:

* No wheat
* No oats
* No dry hop
* Protafloc
* Cold crash

✅ Hallertau provides subtle floral and herbal notes without making the beer hop-forward.

✅ M12 contributes a restrained fruity complexity while allowing the malt character to remain dominant.

✅ The result should resemble a strong continental golden ale with subtle Nordic character from the Kveik fermentation.

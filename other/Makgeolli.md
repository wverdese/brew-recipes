# 🍶 Traditional Homemade Makgeolli (5L batch, sweeter & fuller-bodied)

## Target Stats

* **Final Volume:** ~5 L
* **ABV:** ~4%–5%
* **Final SG:** ~1.030–1.035 (naturally sweeter)
* **Fermentation Temp:** 18–20°C

---

## Ingredients

### Main Fermentation (Day 0)

| Ingredient     | Amount |
| -------------- | ------ |
| Glutinous Rice | 1 kg   |
| Nuruk          | 150 g  |
| Water          | 3.5 L    |

### Deotsul (Rice Addition, Day 3–4)

Choose one of the following:

| Option                       | Amount                              | Characteristics                   |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Option A:** Glutinous Rice | 500 g                               | Creamier, sweeter, thicker body   |
| **Option B:** Normal Rice    | 500 g                               | Lighter body, subtle sweetness    |
| **Option C:** Mixed Rice     | 250 g glutinous + 250 g normal rice | Balanced sweetness, moderate body |

---

## Method

### Day 0: Main Fermentation

#### Preparation:

* Rinse 1 kg glutinous rice 5–7 times until water runs clear.
* Soak rice in room-temperature water for 2 hours.
* Drain rice thoroughly between cloth layers for 30 minutes.

#### Cooking:

* Steam rice in 2 batches (~40 min per batch), lying the rice on cheesecloth in the steamer basket.
* Cool steamed rice thoroughly at room temperature (~30 min).

#### Mixing:

* Sanitize fermenter.
* Mix nuruk (150 g) with half the water (~2 L), soak for 30 min.
* Add cooled rice to fermenter.
* Add nuruk-water mixture; stir gently until absorbed.
* Add remaining water (~1.5L L); stir gently until evenly mixed and submerged.
* Seal fermenter, start fermentation at **18–20°C**.

### Day 1–3

* Stir gently once daily with sanitized spoon.

### Day 4–5

* Seal fermenter and allow to ferment undisturbed.

### Day 5–6 (Primary Straining & Deotsul Addition)

* Strain makgeolli gently to remove spent rice and nuruk residue.
* Steam chosen Deotsul rice, cool it to room temperature.
* Add fresh rice gently to the strained liquid.
* Stir carefully and reseal fermenter.

### Day 7–12

* Ferment sealed and undisturbed at 18–20°C.

### Day 12 (Final Straining & Bottling)

* Strain thoroughly with mesh bag or cheesecloth.
* Bottle immediately in airtight bottles.
* Refrigerate (2–4°C) to halt fermentation.
* Vent bottles daily if carbonation pressure builds.
---

## Conditioning

| Method        | Conditioning Time | Notes                                  |
| ------------- | ----------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Refrigeration | 2–5 days at 2–4°C | Optimal flavor and texture development |

---

## Final Notes

* **Sweeter Makgeolli:** Halting fermentation at higher SG naturally leaves residual sweetness.
* **Body & Texture:** Using Deotsul significantly improves mouthfeel and complexity.
* **Fermentation Temperature:** Cooler fermentation ensures slower, controlled fermentation, preserving sweetness and complexity naturally.

**Chosen Combo (3.5LL water, 150g nuruk, 500g Deotsul rice):**

* High sweetness, medium body, smooth and drinkable, moderate complexity and rich rice flavor.

---

## Variants

* **Stronger ABV Variant:** Increase nuruk to 175g for fuller fermentation and slightly higher alcohol content.
* **Creamier Variant:** Use exclusively glutinous rice (500g) in the Deotsul step.
* **Lighter, More Refreshing Variant:** Increase water slightly (up to 4L total) for a lighter, smoother finish.
